I realized further into writing that this post is more aimed at folks like myself who struggle with ADHD and attentiveness vis-à-vis stimulation. Feel free to continue reading even if this isn't you, but note that the relevance might not be similar.

For many of us converts to Orthodoxy, part of the allure that brought us to the Church was the exposure to a spiritual life that was beautiful–in the highs and the lows, there is always beauty found buried within. Orthodoxy isn't even coy about it; one of the greatest (if not the greatest) compendia of spiritual writing in our tradition, the Philokalia, is literally titled "love of the beautiful." Beauty permeates the life of an Orthodox Christian, and those who have not realized it are nonetheless being pulled into the realization by mere exposure. The iconography adorning our parish walls, the choir joining the celestial hymnody that's endlessly offered to God in praise, adoration, and gratitude, the words of prayer and worship intoned through the services, chanted in like manner unto the ages and ages that have gone before us...

...but the novelty wears off. The realities of the spiritual life quickly catch up with us, whether during our catechumenate, and especially once we're received, where the training wheels come off and we must begin applying everything we've learned to this unseen warfare. For many of us, there's simply no time to stop and smell the flowers; we're fighting the passions, and it seems like they've got a much bigger army than we do. We become familiar with the day-to-day aspects of the spiritual life, prayer rules, fasting, and the services; in a strange myopia, they become another obstacle we must overcome, much like the passions we are fighting.

Add ADHD in the mix (if you're like me) and you've got the perfect storm for the quick checkout lane at the Orthodox Spiritual Life supermarket. Check off our boxes (or at least the ones we find needful enough) and let's keep moving; we've got other things to do.
