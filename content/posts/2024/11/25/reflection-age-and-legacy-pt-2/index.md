### Where do the things that I once enjoyed (and merely maintain at this point) come to an end, as all things do?

As I’ve begun this new chapter of life, with fatherhood and the complexity it adds to nearly every other aspect of life, there have been and continue to be things I’ve had to let go of - for the sake of those around me and their well-being. I can’t say I do a good job of this when I subconsciously and consciously prioritize my wants over others. I am not good at change, especially when demanded (by circumstance) to change in what seems to be the totality of my life. Given my executive dysfunction, I like routine and predictability, especially flexibility. Fatherhood is anything but these.

This results in a long period (at least 8 months long, anyway) where I fight against myself, desperate to preserve the lifestyle in which I’ve found stability. The two pulls bring me to another hypocritical mode of being, where I speak of the ideals and difficulties in, for example, maintaining a social life (I’ll talk about other contexts later on), all the while seeking to jump over to a spur-of-the-moment social event. I complain about the difficulties that the context of fatherhood (one might be inclined to substitute for the word “maturity”) brings, yet happily make impulsive decisions to go do *x* with *y*. I am in a phase of “walking contradiction” currently.

Regarding my social life, I have made some progress in making myself less of a hypocrite. I have set firmer boundaries on how these things must be arranged, but I’ve also foregone social dynamics and structures that I once was an integral piece of. Some of this has felt natural; some of the dynamics I was involved in are trifling in light of the roles I have in life now.

To those who belong to these same dynamics, please forgive me. I don’t have any scorn or disdain, but life goes on, and it will not wait for me with the weight of the obediences I’m under.

This, too, extends to various forms of leisure that I would have been active with before all this. In some sense, I do have the time to partake of them, but even the mere weight of my obligations on my mind suffice in making me deprioritize them. This isn’t to insinuate that I am even prioritizing correctly or always fulfilling the obligations I have in the way I ought. You could say this is part of the problem, and I would not dispute that. However, even if they were all perfectly fulfilled in a moment, I would still be absent from these forms of leisure in exchange for some greater occupation (read: distraction) or to rest and prepare for another day’s workload.

In summary, it is as though I am losing a part of myself. It is for a greater purpose, and I do not deny the need for my sacrifice, but I would be lying to say that it’s been free sailing…

…but where do I draw the line? Where do I place the boundary between frivolous endeavors and things I need to sustain? This is one of the many cruxes that constantly occupy the back of my mind.

Another example would be with my ministry, [BibleBot](https://biblebot.xyz) (and [the non-profit](https://kerygma.digital) by extension). I’ve been in passive burnout with it for a while, with the last significant batch of changes occurring in mid-2021. Since then, it’s mostly in maintenance and adapted to Discord’s developments like threads/forum channels, slash commands, etc. I’ve had the thought linger in the back of my head about whether it’s worthwhile for the service and myself to remain at the helm of the project. What keeps me is the thought that there isn’t anyone I could consciously put forward to work in my stead who could take on the various demands of the position, all the while being able to build upon the bot and take it to greater heights. Dissolution of the project and throwing in the towel isn’t an option when 70,000 communities and ~6 million users rely on us, with the best alternative so remote from our metrics that there is seemingly no suitable replacement for what we do. Do I keep the project in this stasis and hope that something changes? How long do I continue with the ever-growing stagnation I bring to the ministry? Am I resigned to having built something that’s grown seemingly “too big to fail,” but my departure would begin building its coffin? Maybe these questions aren’t tenable in the first place, but this has been on my mind off and on for the past couple of years.

So, to answer this section’s question, I don’t know. For some, what I need to do is very apparent, and some of these are trivial for me to bring to an end. Others, not so much, but the urgency ever grows as I solidify into this chapter of life.
